# About

I’m a technical leader, builder, and communicator with more than 20 years of experience in web development.

My career has taken me through agency work, hands-on engineering, technical architecture, team leadership, and public speaking, but the thread that connects all of it is pretty simple: I care about helping teams build better digital experiences and better ways of working.

Today, I serve as the Director of Web Development at [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu), where I lead the development, maintenance, and growth of the school’s public digital properties, along with the team that supports them.

## My background

I’ve been working in web development since 2003. Over that time, I’ve worked across a wide range of technologies, teams, and challenges, with a large part of my career spent in agency environments.

That agency experience shaped how I think about digital work. It taught me how to work across different organizations, navigate competing priorities, and turn complex needs into practical, scalable solutions. It also taught me that strong outcomes do not come from technology choices alone. They come from communication, trust, clear expectations, solid processes, and teams that know how to work well together.

That perspective still drives the work I do today.

## What I care about

My work sits at the intersection of technical leadership, web platform strategy, and team enablement.

I’m especially interested in:

- building reliable, sustainable digital platforms
- improving development workflows and delivery practices
- helping teams strengthen their approach to testing, quality, and long-term maintainability
- exploring practical, responsible ways to use AI in development work
- sharing lessons through speaking, mentoring, and community involvement

What motivates me most is not just building things. It is helping teams build better systems, better habits, and better ways of working that hold up over time.

## Speaking and community

I’m also an active public speaker and longtime member of the Drupal and open-source community.

My talks often focus on practical topics like modern web development, Drupal, technical leadership, testing, delivery quality, and the responsible use of AI in development workflows. I care a lot about making technical ideas feel clear, useful, and grounded in real-world experience.

You can explore recordings of past talks and upcoming presentations on my [Talks](https://talks.mike-miles.com/) page.

From 2016 to 2020, I hosted the podcast [Developing Up](https://www.developingup.com), which focused on the non-technical side of being a developer and building a strong career in tech.

## Outside of work

Outside of my core professional work, I’m also the creator and maintainer of the book guessing game [Book Baffle](https://www.bookbaffle.com).
