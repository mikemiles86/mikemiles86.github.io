I’m Mike Miles, a technical leader, speaker, and longtime open-source practitioner with a deep interest in how web teams grow, improve, and do their best work.

I currently serve as the Director of Web Development for [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu), where I lead the development, maintenance, and growth of the school’s public digital properties, along with the team that supports them.

[View my talks](https://talks.mike-miles.com/) · [Learn more about MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu)

---

## A little about me

I’ve been working in web development since 2003, and over the years I’ve had the chance to work across a wide range of roles, teams, and challenges.

A large part of my career was spent in agency environments, where I worked with organizations of all sizes to help turn complex needs into practical, scalable digital solutions. That experience taught me that strong digital work is never just about the technology. It’s also about communication, leadership, process, trust, and building systems that can stand up over time.

That perspective continues to shape the work I do today.

---

## What I focus on

My work sits at the intersection of technical leadership, web platform strategy, and team enablement.

I care most about:

- helping teams build reliable, sustainable digital platforms
- improving development workflows and delivery practices
- creating stronger habits around testing, quality, and long-term maintainability
- exploring practical, responsible ways to use AI in development work
- sharing lessons through speaking, community involvement, and conversation

---

## Why this work matters to me

Modern web teams are being asked to do a lot. Move faster. Support more stakeholders. Keep up with constant technical change. Deliver better results with limited time and resources.

The challenge is not just building the thing. It is building the systems, habits, and team culture that make good work possible over the long term.

That is the part of the work I care about most.

## Beyond my day-to-day work

I’m also an active public speaker, and I regularly present on topics such as modern web development, Drupal, technical leadership, testing, delivery quality, and practical AI adoption. You can explore recordings of past talks and upcoming presentations on my [Talks](https://talks.mike-miles.com/) page.

From 2016 to 2020, I hosted the podcast [Developing Up](https://www.developingup.com), which focused on the non-technical side of being a developer and building a strong career in tech.

Outside of my core professional work, I’m also the creator and maintainer of the book guessing game [Book Baffle](https://www.bookbaffle.com).
